## Problem Statement

Millions of hours of college and pro football footage sit dormant in network archives, school A/V departments, and former-players' personal hard drives. The athletes who ran those plays own zero of it. There is no platform that consolidates a football player's career into a single canonical, monetizable, AI-maintained profile — and no platform that connects former teammates economically through their shared on-field history.

The full BLTZ vision is a sports content economy with team-based revenue share. The MVP wedge is much smaller: be the place where every college, pro, and former-pro football player has a beautiful, AI-generated, auto-updating "locker" that aggregates their career footage and statistics into one canonical page they own and can share.

## Demand Evidence

User self-reported **soft signals only** (D1 = B). No specific named individuals have committed money, time, or asked when it ships. This is the most important gap in the project today. The design and the assignment are both shaped around closing it.

What the founder *does* have, which most pre-traction founders do not:

- **Personal credibility** — former professional football player, UC Berkeley alum
- **Inventory access** — direct access to archived footage through his school and team
- **Network** — peer relationships with athletes who could become the first 50 lockers
- **Conviction + craft** — produced a 60+ feature spec doc independently before this session

These convert "no demand evidence" into "uniquely positioned to generate demand evidence in 2-3 weeks."

## Status Quo

What dead inventory looks like today:

- **Former pros** post highlight reels on personal Instagram accounts that get suppressed by the algorithm 3 years post-retirement. Career footage scattered across YouTube uploads from random fans, school archives they can't access, NFL Films licensing they don't control.
- **Current college players** rely on Hudl (coach-controlled, ugly, no public profile, no ownership) plus a personal Twitter/X feed they curate manually.
- **Schools and conferences** sit on terabytes of game film with no distribution strategy beyond a cobwebbed YouTube channel and the occasional "Top 10 plays of the decade" promo cut.
- **Fans** have no way to discover lesser-known players from any era. A former All-Pac-10 lineman in 2008 is essentially invisible online.

The cost of the workaround: athletes' marketable identity decays the moment they leave the game. Schools' inventory depreciates to zero. Fans' nostalgic demand has no place to spend.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

User D2 = B (archetype not named individual). The archetype:

- Former pro or current college football player, age 19–55
- Has documented game footage somewhere (school archive, network B-roll, his own GoPro, fan uploads, NFL Films)
- Cares about his post-football identity, brand, or financial second act
- Has not been able to get his own career consolidated anywhere because no tool exists

**Narrowest wedge selected (D3 = B, refined by founder context):** AI Athlete Locker, web-first, no money infrastructure. Free profiles auto-built from web data plus founder-supplied archive footage.

## Constraints

- Founder time is the binding resource. Every week not spent on customer evidence is a week wasted.
- Footage rights vary by source: founder's personal archive (clean), school archive (needs partnership), network B-roll (needs licensing). The MVP only uses founder-clean and athlete-uploaded footage to avoid legal entanglement before product-market fit.
- No Stripe, no payouts, no team-share math at MVP. Adding money infrastructure before audience is the most common cause of death in creator-economy startups.

## Premises

Agreed:

- **P1.** Team-revenue-share is the long-term moat. AI Locker is the wedge. Money layers on after density.
- **P2.** Geography- or cohort-density-locked launch. For this MVP, the cohort is "founder's personal network" rather than a county.
- **P3.** Web-first MVP. Recruiters, parents, fans, and schools all live on desktop. Mobile-native in v2 once usage demands it.
- **P4.** "AI builds the profile in 30 seconds" is the magic moment. Everything else is supporting cast.
- **P5.** No payments / NIL / Stripe until 1,000 lockers are live in one cohort.

Rejected with reasoning (founder signal):

- **P6 (rejected).** The original brief targeted high school athletes, which would have triggered FERPA, COPPA, parental consent, and minor-NIL complexity. Founder rejected the HS focus and reframed the MVP to college, current pro, and former pro players. All adults. Legal surface drops by ~80%. This was the single most valuable decision of the session.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Founder-Network Concierge MVP
Manually build 50–100 lockers for athletes the founder knows personally. Hand-curate footage, AI-polish bios and timelines, ship as web pages. 4–6 weeks. Web stack. Zero compliance complexity.

### Approach B: Berkeley Athletics Pilot Partnership
B2B deal with UC Berkeley Athletics. School grants archive access. Build "Cal Football Locker" covering players from 2010–2025. Revenue-share with the AD. Use as case study to sell the next 5 schools. 6–10 weeks of product plus an unpredictable deal cycle.

### Approach C: Former Pros Only — Adult Marketplace
Skip college. Build for retired NFL/CFL/XFL players. Career-highlight collector cards as the first transaction. Team-reunion economy as the emotional hook. 8–10 weeks. Niche but high-ARPU.

### Approach D (selected): Hybrid — A in build, B in parallel sales motion
Ship Approach A as the artifact (4–6 weeks) while simultaneously initiating the Berkeley conversation in week 1. The build cycle and deal cycle do not block each other. By the time the product is ready to demo, the meeting is warm. By the time the meeting happens, the product is ready to demo.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach D.** The build motion (A) and the sales motion (B) run on parallel tracks.

### Build track (A) — Weeks 1–6

Week 1–2: Stack + skeleton
- Next.js 14 (App Router) + Tailwind. Hosted on Vercel.
- Sanity or Payload CMS for locker content (so non-engineers can edit/extend).
- Mux for video hosting + transcoding + adaptive streaming.
- Claude API for bio generation, career timeline synthesis, highlight ranking from raw footage.
- Postgres (Supabase) for user accounts and locker metadata.
- One canonical `/locker/{slug}` route. SEO-clean. Open Graph image generation per locker.

Week 2–4: First 10 lockers, hand-built
- Founder picks 10 athletes from his network (mix: former pro teammates, Cal alums, position coaches).
- For each: collect footage, run through AI pipeline, hand-polish, ship.
- Each locker has: hero reel, career timeline, stats, "Believer Since" placeholder (no money flow yet — just a name-drop list of fans), share buttons, ownership claim mechanism.
- Athletes get a "Claim This Locker" link and admin access to edit.

Week 4–6: Self-serve onboarding + next 40
- Build a 3-screen onboarding: name + school + position → AI fetches everything → athlete reviews and approves.
- Open private signup link to next 40 athletes from the founder's extended network.
- Track: time-to-first-share, claim rate, athlete-invited-teammate rate.

### Sales track (B) — Weeks 1–10 (parallel)

Week 1: Founder books meeting with UC Berkeley Athletic Director's office through alumni channels.
Week 2–3: Discovery meeting. Show wireframe + 1–2 founder-built lockers.
Week 4–6: Show the live product, propose pilot terms (1 year, revenue-share, full archive access, exclusive on Cal football lockers for 12 months).
Week 7–10: Negotiate paper. Soft-launch Cal locker microsite.

### What is *explicitly not* in the MVP

- No Stripe, no payouts, no W-9 collection.
- No high school athletes.
- No fan/scout side. (Fans can view lockers and "follow" — no points, no ranks, no Believer Since money mechanic.)
- No coach dashboard. No NIL compliance engine.
- No mobile native app.
- No team-pool math, no atomic payout splits, no fraud scoring pipeline.

These all come back later. None of them are needed to prove the wedge.

## Open Questions

1. **Footage rights at scale.** Founder's personal archive is clean. Cal's archive is contingent on the pilot deal. NFL Films-licensed footage will require a separate licensing motion — out of scope for MVP but needs a long-lead conversation.
2. **The "Claim This Locker" UX for unsolicited profiles.** If you build a locker for someone who hasn't asked, what's the consent + takedown flow? Even with adults, an athlete should be able to claim, edit, or remove their locker in one click.
3. **Fan-side at MVP — really nothing?** There's a case for a single-button "Follow" + an email digest as the only fan feature. Adds zero technical complexity, captures email, and validates fan demand without building the full Scout economy.
4. **Berkeley deal: revenue-share or flat license?** Flat license is faster to close. Revenue-share is more aligned long-term. The right answer probably depends on what the AD office's incentive structure is.
5. **AI footage tagging accuracy.** Auto-cutting highlights from raw game film requires player tracking. This is a real ML problem at production quality. For MVP, manual cuts + AI bio/timeline only. Production-grade auto-highlight is a v2 problem.

## Success Criteria

End of Week 10:

- Decision point: ship money infrastructure (P5 unlock), or keep growing audience.

## Distribution Plan

- Distribution channel: organic web. SEO-optimized locker pages. Open Graph images that look great in iMessage / Twitter / LinkedIn.
- Acquisition: founder shares lockers in his personal network. Each locker is its own marketing artifact.
- Hosting: Vercel (web) + Mux (video) + Supabase (data). All managed services. No DevOps burn.
- CI/CD: Vercel auto-deploy on merge to main. Preview deploys per PR for the founder to share with athletes before publish.

No mobile distribution at MVP. No app store gates.

## Dependencies

- Mux account + payment method (Mux's free tier covers the first ~50 lockers).
- Supabase project (free tier covers MVP).

## Engineering Review Decisions (added 2026-04-24 by /plan-eng-review)

- **A1 Claim flow:** magic-link email + Supabase Auth. Single link unlocks edit + takedown.
- **A2 Footage provenance:** every `Clip` row carries `source_provenance` enum (`founder_archive | cal_archive | athlete_uploaded | fan_uploaded`) + linked `Consent` record (granter, scope, timestamp) + visible provenance badge on locker UI from day 1.
- **A3 Magic-moment pipeline:** hybrid auto-draft → founder approval → athlete-only preview → publish. Sources: YouTube, Wikipedia, ESPN, college site, NFL team site. Athlete sees the <30s magic on first view post-approval.
- **T1 Hallucination gate:** every numeric claim in Claude-generated bio cross-references against scraped sources; mismatches flag the draft for founder.
- **P1 Pipeline cost:** two-stage. Custom HTTP scrapers (cheerio/playwright) extract structured data → Gemini 2.5 Flash (or GPT-5 Nano) synthesizes bio + timeline. Claude reserved for founder-facing polish only. ~70% cost reduction vs single-LLM pipeline.
- **P2 Perf gates (week 1):** OG image cached at edge (generate on publish, store in Vercel Blob); Claude API hard cost ceiling (50k tokens/draft, 3 retries max); Mux bandwidth alarm at 80% of free tier; Locker page TTFB <800ms p95 enforced by CI Lighthouse on preview deploys.

## Design Review Decisions (added 2026-04-24 by /plan-design-review)

Design system source of truth lives in [DESIGN.md](../../../bltz/.claude/worktrees/busy-hellman-6b7937/DESIGN.md) at repo root. Decisions made in review:

- **D1 Locker hero:** full-viewport (`100vh`) with muted autoplay HLS clip via Mux behind dark gradient. Athlete name in gold (`#F5A623`) Barlow Condensed 120px desktop / 96px mobile.
- **D2 Magic-moment loader:** live discovery log via SSE — show pipeline working in real time ("Found 47 plays from 2018 Cal vs Stanford..."). The wait IS the magic.
- **D3 State coverage:** full matrix written for locker page, onboarding, magic-moment reveal, founder queue, edit interface, claim landing. Every state has loading / empty / error / success / partial defined. Empty states use warm copy + primary action.
- **D4 Design system tokens:** color, type, scale, spacing, radius, motion all locked in DESIGN.md. Brand: gold accent for athlete name + peak stats, blue for actions, navy bg, Barlow Condensed display + JetBrains Mono numerics.
- **D5 Anti-patterns blocked:** no purple, no centered card grids, no icon-in-colored-circles, no decorative blobs, no emoji, no pill buttons, no `system-ui` fallback, no carousels for the highlight reel. Editorial asymmetric layout for clip grid.
- **D6 Believer Since v0:** pre-seed with founder + 1-2 known teammates so the section feels alive on first view.
- **D7 OG card template:** scoped into MVP week 1 (the marketing engine — every share is a billboard). Hero clip thumb + name in gold + position/school + provenance footer.
- **D8 Founder queue UX:** designed properly now (not janky). Keyboard nav, side-by-side preview, batch approve.
- **D9 Responsive:** mobile is intentional, not "stacked desktop." Hero name 120→96px, timeline rotates horizontal→vertical, reel goes 1+4 → 1+2 + see more.
- **D10 A11y:** body text ≥16px, contrast ≥4.5:1, touch targets ≥44px, focus rings 2px gold offset 2px, captions on hero clips, reduced-motion fallback.
